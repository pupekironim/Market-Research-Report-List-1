<p><p>The pediatric spinal orthoses market is divided into two main types: dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing some mobility, making them suitable for active children or those undergoing rehabilitation. In contrast, static orthotics focus on providing rigid support to correct spinal alignment or prevent deformities, restricting movement to facilitate healing. Both types aim to improve postural control and overall spinal health for children with various conditions affecting their spine.</p></p>
