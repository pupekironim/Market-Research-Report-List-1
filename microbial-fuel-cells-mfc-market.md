<p><p>Microbial Fuel Cells (MFC) are innovative bioelectrochemical systems that convert organic matter into electricity using microorganisms. The market is divided into Mediator and Mediator-free MFC types. Mediator MFCs utilize electron-shuttling compounds to enhance electron transfer between microbes and the anode, resulting in higher power output. In contrast, Mediator-free MFCs rely on direct electron transfer, offering advantages like reduced costs and simpler designs, although they may have lower efficiency. Both types are integral for sustainable energy applications and wastewater treatment.</p></p>
